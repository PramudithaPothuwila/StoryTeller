## Creative Basics
- Genre: grounded detective mystery built for a branching RPG investigation.
- Tone: rain-soaked, tense, procedural, morally wary.
- Core premise: a quiet former railway maintenance engineer is found dead in an abandoned station, but the physical scene says the station was chosen after the killing.
- Player role: Detective Arden Pike, an outside investigator who has to read place, motive, and staged evidence.
- Main conflict: prove why the body was moved before the redevelopment board demolishes the station and erases the hidden record.
- Setting: Black Hollow, a rail town whose abandoned station is part shrine, part liability.
- Ending style: truth-with-cost, with a playable false-frame ending if the player follows the obvious suspect.

## Clue Chain
- Drag marks contradict the initial station-as-murder-site assumption.
- Fresh footprints create a red herring around Jessa Holt.
- Platform Marker 7 points to an old safety file and makes Silas look symbolically guilty.
- Silas can reveal the Locker 12 key if approached with evidence rather than accusation.
- The Signal Room Ledger explains what Rowan knew and why Evelyn needed the station searched.

## Suspect Structure
- Evelyn Park: real killer, public civic reformer, private cover-up custodian.
- Silas Crowe: obvious frame target, former signalman, keeps the station's memory alive.
- Jessa Holt: trespasser whose footprints muddy the crime scene but also prove someone moved through after the body dump.

## Gameplay Spine
- Start at the staged body scene.
- Let the player choose between physical evidence and station lore.
- Reward concrete observation with the key and ledger.
- Let incomplete proof produce a plausible but wrong ending.
